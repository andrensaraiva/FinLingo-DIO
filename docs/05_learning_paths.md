# 5. Learning Path Structure

## Overview
FinLingo's MVP includes **7 learning tracks**, each with 4–5 micro-lessons. Tracks are ordered by difficulty and relevance to everyday financial life.

---

## Track 1: 💳 Credit Card Basics

**Goal:** Understand what credit cards are, how they work, and how to use them responsibly.

### Lessons
1. **What Is a Credit Card?** — How it differs from debit, how the billing cycle works
2. **Interest Rates and Minimum Payments** — What APR means, why minimum payments are a trap
3. **Using Credit Cards Wisely** — Best practices, building credit, avoiding debt spirals

### Quiz Ideas
- *"If your credit card balance is $1,000 and you only pay the minimum of $25/month at 20% APR, approximately how long will it take to pay it off?"* (Multiple choice)
- *True or False: "A credit card gives you free money that you don't have to pay back."*

### Sample AI Question
> "Fino, what happens if I miss a credit card payment?"

---

## Track 2: 📊 Interest and Installments

**Goal:** Understand how interest works — simple vs. compound, and how installment plans affect total cost.

### Lessons
1. **What Is Interest?** — The concept of borrowing cost, lender's perspective
2. **Simple vs. Compound Interest** — The math made simple, with real-world examples
3. **Installment Plans Explained** — How splitting payments changes total cost

### Quiz Ideas
- *"You invest $1,000 at 5% compound interest for 3 years. Which formula applies?"* (Multiple choice)
- *Fill in the blank: "Compound interest is calculated on the ______ plus accumulated interest."*

### Sample AI Question
> "Can you explain compound interest like I'm 15?"

---

## Track 3: 🛡️ Emergency Fund

**Goal:** Learn why an emergency fund matters and how to start one realistically.

### Lessons
1. **What Is an Emergency Fund?** — Why it exists, what counts as an emergency
2. **How Much Should You Save?** — The 3–6 month rule, and how to adjust it to your reality
3. **How to Start Saving Today** — Practical micro-saving strategies, automation tips

### Quiz Ideas
- *"Which of these is a good reason to use your emergency fund?"* (Multiple choice: job loss, new phone, vacation, concert tickets)
- *True or False: "Your emergency fund should be invested in high-risk stocks for maximum growth."*

### Sample AI Question
> "I earn $1,500/month. How much should my emergency fund be?"

---

## Track 4: 🏦 Understanding Loans

**Goal:** Demystify loans — types, terms, interest, and how to evaluate whether borrowing makes sense.

### Lessons
1. **What Is a Loan?** — Types of loans, secured vs. unsecured
2. **Loan Terms You Must Know** — Principal, interest rate, term, amortization
3. **Should You Take That Loan?** — A framework for evaluating borrowing decisions
4. **The Real Cost of a Loan** — Total interest paid, early repayment, hidden fees

### Quiz Ideas
- *"Which type of loan typically has a lower interest rate?"* (Secured / Unsecured / Both the same / Depends on your zodiac sign 😄)
- *"Calculate: If you borrow $5,000 at 10% simple interest for 2 years, how much total interest do you pay?"*

### Sample AI Question
> "Is it better to pay off a loan early or invest the extra money?"

---

## Track 5: 💸 Spending Control

**Goal:** Learn how to track, categorize, and reduce unnecessary spending.

### Lessons
1. **Where Does Your Money Go?** — Tracking expenses, fixed vs. variable costs
2. **The 50/30/20 Rule** — A simple budgeting framework
3. **Cutting Costs Without Suffering** — Practical tips, the latte factor, subscription audits
4. **Smart Spending Habits** — Waiting periods, wish lists, needs vs. wants

### Quiz Ideas
- *"According to the 50/30/20 rule, how much of your income should go to savings?"* (10% / 20% / 30% / 50%)
- *True or False: "Cutting small daily expenses can save over $1,000 per year."*

### Sample AI Question
> "How do I figure out the difference between a need and a want?"

---

## Track 6: 🔒 Digital Banking Safety

**Goal:** Understand how to protect yourself in the digital financial world.

### Lessons
1. **Common Financial Scams** — Phishing, fake links, social engineering
2. **Protecting Your Accounts** — Strong passwords, 2FA, biometrics
3. **Safe Online Transactions** — What to check before buying online, secure payment methods

### Quiz Ideas
- *"You receive an email saying your bank account is locked. What should you do?"* (Click the link / Call your bank directly / Reply with your password / Ignore if unsolicited)
- *True or False: "Using public Wi-Fi to check your bank account is always safe."*

### Sample AI Question
> "How do I know if a banking email is real or a scam?"

---

## Track 7: 🌱 Intro to Investing

**Goal:** Understand the very basics of investing — what it is, common types, and how to start thinking about it.

### Lessons
1. **What Is Investing?** — Saving vs. investing, risk vs. return
2. **Common Investment Types** — Savings accounts, bonds, stocks, index funds (high-level)
3. **Getting Started Safely** — How to think about risk tolerance, starting small, long-term mindset

### Quiz Ideas
- *"Which investment type generally has the highest risk AND highest potential return?"* (Savings account / Government bond / Individual stocks / Index fund)
- *True or False: "Investing always guarantees you won't lose money."*

### Sample AI Question
> "What's the simplest way to start investing with $50?"
